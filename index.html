Our Founder

Meet Madam Zulfat Mukarubega

A visionary leader, genocide survivor, and champion for youth, women, and people with disabilities. She turned personal struggles into a mission to uplift others, believing one opportunity can change a life, a family, and a community.

Vision & Mission

Mission: To break the cycle of poverty by empowering disadvantaged youth, women, and people with disabilities through education, vocational training, and entrepreneurship.

Vision: A Rwanda where every individual has the opportunity to achieve self-reliance and financial independence.

Inspiration & Journey

Born from the need created by Rwanda’s 1994 Genocide, Madam Zulfat’s journey is one of resilience and transformation. Her personal experiences have inspired her to work relentlessly for those left without hope.

Celebrating Achievements

Under her leadership, over 387 students have been sponsored for education, 145 individuals placed in international jobs, and hundreds of local jobs created through vocational training.

Words from the Founder

"Every act of kindness creates a ripple effect. I believe that by empowering one person, we uplift an entire community."

Who We Are

Our Mission

Our Vision

Our Values

Our History

Born out of the devastation of the 1994 Genocide, Zulfat Foundation was founded in 2008 to provide dignity and opportunity for those left behind.

Our Team

A passionate group of professionals dedicated to transforming lives.

Community Impact

We have transformed hundreds of lives by creating local jobs and supporting people with disabilities with essential needs, education, and mentorship.
